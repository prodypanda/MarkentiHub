## 2026-05-16 - [crypto.timingSafeEqual Length Mismatch DoS]
**Vulnerability:** Node.js `crypto.timingSafeEqual` throws an unhandled exception (`ERR_CRYPTO_TIMING_SAFE_EQUAL_LENGTH`) when the two buffers provided have different lengths. In `verifyWebhookSignature` in `pandamarket/backend/src/utils/crypto.ts`, user-provided signatures of arbitrary lengths were directly converted to buffers and passed to this function. This allows an attacker to cause a Denial of Service (DoS) by sending webhook requests with a signature of an incorrect length, crashing the backend server.
**Learning:** Functions designed for security often have strict preconditions. `crypto.timingSafeEqual` trades flexibility for constant-time execution to prevent timing attacks. It assumes lengths are pre-validated.
**Prevention:** Always verify that buffer lengths are equal before calling `crypto.timingSafeEqual`. Since expected hash lengths (like SHA-256 HMAC) are constant and known, rejecting differing lengths early via an explicit length check doesn't introduce meaningful timing vulnerabilities, but effectively prevents the unhandled exception crash.
